Reviews for 'The Edge Chronicles' Series

Emily May

Oh my god, what an entertaining and enjoyable read about adventure, hope and never giving up even in the most dire circumstances.

4/5

Phrynne

One thing I will say for this author is that he is very good at making up magical creatures with amazing names and his illustrator is equally good at drawing them! The picture of Mag, the terrible termagant trog, straight after her change has to be seen to be believed.

4/5

Gavin Hetherington

A childhood favourite of mine that I just re-read for the first time in twenty years and I adore it still! I am so relieved I didn't think it was not as good as when I was a child, as that has happened with re-reading some of my childhood favourites.

5/5

Christina H.

This is one of those series, like the famous Harry Potter, and like the Chrestomanci series of Diana Wynne Jones, that I used to fervently and desperately wish were real. Or rather, that felt so real incredibly real that I simply couldn't believe they weren't.

5/5

Cindy E.

I was really surprised about this series. Not because of the fact that it was bad. It was actually better then I thought it would be. The thing that surprised me is that this is considered a children's series and the rather disturbing stuff that goes on in here.

4/5

Thibault Busschots

What a blast of nostalgic fun! Now I remember why I used to love these books so much when I was a kid.

4/5

Sandi

The book graphics are outstanding. Worth the read for that alone. This is a YA series. Good for cleaning your palette and tickling your funny bone.

3/5

Ryan R.

Very imaginative and moody. The setting is dark and colourful and a little bit twisted, with frequent gross-out moments to appeal to the target audience. However, Many of the scenes appeared to randomly follow the last one, and a lot of bizarre stuff occurs for no given reason.

3/5
Adam Southwick. 2023.
